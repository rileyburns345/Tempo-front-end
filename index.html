Using Tempo

How to gain Ovulation Intelligence by using Tempo:

Your Morning Routine

Every morning, measure your basal body temperature and record it using Tempo. Use a thermometer specific to this purpose that is accurate to 2 decimal places. Measure your temperature before getting out of bed each morning, as movement will raise your body temperature above basal body temp.


Understanding The Risks

Tempo uses the "Temperature Method" which detects changes in basal body temperature as an indication of ovulation. This can help predict a fertile window during which to avoid sex or use protection in order to prevent pregnancy. Planned Parenthood states this method is 78-88% effective, assuming each cycle is like the last, the protection that is used does not fail, and that the method is used perfectly every time. Because each person is different and use is likely to be somewhat imperfect, actuall effectiveness may deviate from this rate. For this reason, Tempo should be used only to gain Ovulation Intellegence and is recommended as a supplement to other forms of birth control to prevent pregnancy effectively. This method does not protect against STI's (Sexually Transmitted Infections).


Factors That Impact Accuracy

For most accurate use, be aware of other factors that may impact your basal body temperature such as alcohol use, certain medications, or sickness. When these are a factor, it is recommended that you do not input your body temperature data for that day.


Tempo's Method May Not Be Right For You

Do not use this method if you have a medical condition where pregnancy would be associated with a significant risk to your health, if you are taking hormonal birth control, if you have highly irregular cycles, or if you are post menopausal.
